What AI actually is for small business

AI tools — like ChatGPT, Claude, and similar — are software that can understand and generate text, answer questions, and help with tasks that previously required a human.

For small businesses, the practical applications are things like: a chat widget on your website that answers common customer questions 24/7, a tool that drafts your email replies so you just edit and send, or software that summarises long documents in seconds.

None of this requires you to be technical. I identify where AI genuinely saves time in your specific business, set it up, and train your team to use it.

The honest truth about AI

AI is genuinely useful — but it's being massively over-hyped. Before anyone invests in AI tools, I want them to understand:

  • AI makes mistakes — always review its output
  • It's a tool that helps people, not a replacement for them
  • The simpler the task, the more reliably it works
  • Some businesses will benefit a lot, some very little
  • Implementation takes thought — not just a subscription

Being Honest

What AI is NOT good for

I'd rather tell you the truth upfront than oversell something that won't work for you.

❌ Replacing your team

AI is a tool that helps people work faster. It doesn't replace the relationships, judgement, and expertise your staff bring.

❌ Making important decisions

AI can inform decisions but shouldn't make them. Legal, financial, medical, or safety decisions need human judgement.

❌ Unsupervised customer contact

AI chatbots need oversight. They can give wrong answers confidently. Always review and test thoroughly before deploying.

❌ Overnight transformation

Good AI implementation takes planning. It's not plug-and-play. Budget time for setup, testing, and staff training.
