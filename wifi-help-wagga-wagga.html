Why Is My WiFi Bad?

Common causes of bad WiFi

Bad WiFi almost always has a fixable cause. Here are the most common ones I see in Wagga Wagga homes and businesses:

  • Router placement — A router tucked in a cupboard or behind a TV is never going to give great coverage. Position matters a lot.
  • Outdated equipment — Routers from 5+ years ago are often the problem. Modern WiFi 6 routers are dramatically better.
  • Interference from other devices — Microwaves, baby monitors, and neighbouring WiFi networks all cause interference.
  • Too many devices on one band — A busy 2.4GHz band slows everything down. Proper configuration helps.
  • ISP issues — Sometimes the problem is upstream with your internet provider. I can help identify this too.

Before you call

Try these first — they fix about 20% of WiFi problems:

  • Restart your router (unplug for 30 seconds)
  • Move closer to the router to test
  • Check if the problem is on all devices or just one

If none of that works, give me a call.
