What is Automation?

Stop doing it manually

Automation means getting your software to do repetitive tasks automatically — so you don't have to. Every time you do the same task twice, there's a good chance a computer can do it for you.

I'm not talking about robots or complicated software. Most small business automation is simple: if a customer fills in a form, automatically send them a confirmation email. If a new job comes in, automatically add it to your calendar. If an invoice hasn't been paid, automatically send a reminder.

Small automations like these can save hours every week — and they run whether you're in the office or not.

If you've ever said any of these…

  • "I spend so much time on admin"
  • "I keep forgetting to follow up with clients"
  • "I copy the same thing into two different places every day"
  • "I wish this would just happen automatically"
  • "I'm the only one who knows how to do this"

…then automation is probably worth a conversation.

Real Examples

What automation looks like in practice

Here are some of the most common automations I set up for Wagga Wagga businesses.

📬

Booking Confirmations

Customer makes a booking → they automatically receive a confirmation email and reminder the day before. No manual work required.

💰

Invoice Reminders

Invoice goes unpaid → automatic reminder sent at 7 days, 14 days, 30 days. Chasing money without the awkward phone calls.

🔄

Email Follow-ups

New enquiry comes in → automatic follow-up sequence kicks off. You stay top of mind without lifting a finger.

📋

Form to CRM

Website contact form submitted → lead automatically added to your CRM or spreadsheet, no copy-paste required.

📱

Social Media Scheduling

Write your posts once, schedule them for the whole month. Stay active without logging in every day.

🔔

Inventory & Stock Alerts

Stock falls below a threshold → you get an automatic alert. Order before you run out, not after.

Tools I Use

The right tool for the job

Zapier

The most popular automation platform. Connects 5,000+ apps. Great for simple, reliable automations between common business tools.

Make (formerly Integromat)

More powerful than Zapier for complex workflows. Better value at scale. I use this for multi-step automations with logic and conditions.

Microsoft Power Automate

Built into Microsoft 365. If you're already using M365, this is often the most seamless way to automate within your existing tools.

WordPress Plugins

For businesses with a WordPress site, many automations can be done directly through plugins — no third-party platform needed.
